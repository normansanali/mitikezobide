Ryan Michael Gentles (born November 28, 1977) is the manager of The Strokes, a New York City-based band, as well as the CEO of Wiz Kid Management. He was the singer and guitarist in two bands before joining the Strokes: Timmsie’s First Time, which disbanded in 1995, and The Selzers, which disbanded in 2000. Matthew Romano, The Strokes’ drum technician and back-up drummer, played drums in both bands. Ryan recently said that being a member of the Selzers “wasn’t exactly a proud moment” for him.  When Gentles was booking acts for the Mercury Lounge in New York, he encountered the Strokes. He was so taken aback by their performance that he left his job to become their manager. He is now often referred to as “the sixth Stroke.” In their music video “The End Has No End,” he had a leading part. The Strokes’ earnings are divided six ways, with Gentles getting the same amount as the other members of the band. The song Gentles is on Albert Hammond, Jr.’s album Yours to Keep. He considers his father to be his idol, according to his MySpace profile. In January 2008, he combined his firm with that of Danny Goldberg, a music industry veteran, to create Gold Village/Wiz Kid Management.
