Chris Evans children-Hollywood star, Chris Evans was born on June 13th, 1981 in Boston, Massachusetts in the United States of America.

He was born to Bob, a dentist and Lisa who was an artistic director at the Concord Youth Theatre.

He attended the acting camp as a kid and liked musical theater. In the musical Bye Bye Birdie, he portrayed Randolph MacAfee. During Christmastime, he and his siblings would often perform in front of family members; he recalled that being on stage “felt like home.”

Evans spent the summer in New York City where he attended studies at the Lee Strasberg Theatre and Film Institute before beginning his senior year of high school. In 1999, Evans earned a diploma from Lincoln-Sudbury Regional High School. He attended the same school as Jeremy Strong.

Chris Evans who is famous for his role as Captain America in the famous Marvel Superhero film “Avengers” started his acting career by playing parts in TV shows like Opposite Sex in 2000.

He earned notoriety for his portrayal of the Marvel Comics character the Human Torch in Fantastic Four (2005) and Fantastic Four: Rise of the Silver Surfer after making cameos in a number of teen movies, such as 2001’s Not Another Teen Movie (2007).

Evans’ portrayal of Steve Rogers/Captain America across several Marvel Studios films, including Captain America: The First Avenger (2011) and Avengers: Endgame, contributed to his rise to prominence in 2019. Thanks to his work in the franchise, he rose to become one of the highest-paid actors in the world.

Evans has appeared in other dramas like Gifted (2017), the mystery movie Knives Out (2019), and the television miniseries Defending Jacob in addition to his work in comic books (2020).

With the love drama Before We Go, which he also produced and featured in, he made his directing debut in 2014. Evans made his Broadway debut in the Lobby Hero revival in 2018, for which he was nominated for a Drama League Award.
