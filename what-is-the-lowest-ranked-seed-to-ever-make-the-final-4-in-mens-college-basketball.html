The Significance of Seeding in the NCAA Tournament

The NCAA Tournament committee introduced seeding back in 1979, aiming to provide a fair and structured path for teams based on their regular-season performance.

The seeding system categorizes teams from No. 1 to No. 16, with the No. 1 seeds traditionally having the easiest route to the Final Four.

While no path is easy, the seeding system aims to reward teams for their regular-season accomplishments and sets the stage for captivating matchups and unpredictable outcomes.
