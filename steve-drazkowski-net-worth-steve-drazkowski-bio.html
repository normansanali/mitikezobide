Introduction of Steve Drazkowski | Steve Drazkowski Bio

Steve Drazkowski is an American politician serving as a member of the Minnesota Senate. He represents District 20 in the state.

His district includes all of Wabasha County. It also comprises significant portions of Olmsted County, Goodhue County, and Winona County, along with a smaller section of Dakota County.

Prior to his role in the Senate, he held a position in the Minnesota House of Representatives.

Steve Drazkowski Early Life

Steve Drazkowski was born on November 27, 1964, in Winona, Minnesota. He grew up on a farm in Bluff Siding, Wisconsin. His parents’ names are not known.

Steve Drazkowski Education

He graduated from Cochrane-Fountain City High School. In 1989, he earned a Bachelor of Science in Agriculture from the University of Wisconsin-River Falls.

Later, he majored in agricultural economics at the University of Minnesota, where he also obtained his bachelor’s degree in 1994.

He worked as a farmer and business owner after graduating from college before going into politics.

Steve Drazkowski Career

Drazkowski has been involved in various roles. He served as a Minnesota Department of Natural Resources firearms safety instructor.

Additionally, he volunteered with Wabasha County 4-H. He was also the former president of the Minnesota Forage and Grassland Council.

In his political journey, he ran unsuccessfully for the District 28 seat in the Minnesota Senate in 2006. However, he was elected to the Minnesota House of Representatives.

The election took place in a special election held on August 7, 2007, after Representative Steve Sviggum resigned. Since then, he has been reelected in 2008, 2010, 2012, 2014, 2016, 2018, and 2020.

In 2010, Drazkowski introduced legislation inspired by Arizona’s controversial immigration law. In 2012, he proposed an Employee Freedom Constitutional Amendment.

Its aim was to include a right-to-work clause in the Minnesota Constitution, with the goal of reducing union influence.

In early 2019, Drazkowski referred U.S. Representative Ilhan Omar to the Minnesota Campaign Finance and Public Disclosure Board. He cited alleged violations of election ethics rules.

On December 7, 2018, Drazkowski and three other Republican state representatives formed the New Republican House Caucus.

Despite forming the new caucus, they continued to uphold their commitment to Republican ideals.

During the 2019 session, they introduced various bills centered around constitutional matters. They also presented their own budget proposal.

In a 2023 debate over a bill for free breakfast and lunch for Minnesota students, Drazkowski made controversial remarks. He questioned the existence of hunger in Minnesota.

His remarks went viral on Twitter and received national attention. However, State Senator Heather Gustafson responded to his claims.

She stated that a significant number of students in Drazkowski’s district qualified for free and reduced lunch.
