THE BACHELORETTE is back for another season of love games as 25 new men line up for a chance to win Charity Lawson's affection.

Xavier Bonner is one of the contestants featured in the show's 20th season and fans already think they know how far he's gotten in the competition.

Who is The Bachelorette's Xavier Bonner?

Hailing from Carrboro, North Carolina, Xavier Bonner is a 27-year-old biomedical scientist known for appearing on The Bachelorette's 20th season.

The dating show describes the six-foot-six contestant as "one tall glass of water" who also "has the smarts and charisma to match."

Xavier is a graduate of both Morehouse College and the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill.

While he was enrolled at UNC-Chapel Hill, he was a part of the school's Diversity and Student Success program.

He also co-founded the institution's Society for Black Biomedical Scientists.

"I want to build a bridge for black students who want to be a consultant, venture capitalist, or pursue other opportunities that are inside the biotech sphere," the reality star explained.

Regarding his diversity initiatives, he told Carolina Graduate School Magazine: "Everybody deserves a shot, no matter your skin color or background.

"We live in a world where now, everyone has more access, and there might be younger generations who know that they can become a scientist because of the space you take up now as a researcher and through volunteer work."

How far does Xavier get in The Bachelorette season 20?

At the beginning of the 20th season, Bachelor Nation sleuths think Xavier will make the show's top four.

In fact, Xavier made it to the top three before being eliminated during fantasy suites week in Fiji.

During a dinner, Xavier revealed he had been unfaithful in his past relationships.

As Charity had been cheated on before, this triggered her resulting in her decision to eliminate Xavier.

In a confessional, Charity said she was willing to hear him out after admitting his past mistakes.

Charity said: "Xavier dropped a huge bomb. I've experienced infidelity on and off for about six years and it literally breaks you.

"There's nothing worse in this world than betrayal with the person you love. How I feel about Xavier, I want it to be a forever thing. I feel like I owe it to us to hear him out."

Upon hearing Xavier out, he dropped a bomb admitting he wasn't sure he could be the man Charity is expecting him to be.

In this awkward exchange, Xavier said: "In my heart, there’s no f**king doubt.

"But in my mind, there is that doubt. And for me to get to that point, it has to happen tonight."

Charity was extremely upset by his answer and stormed out, later eliminating the North Carolina native.
