Is Fortnite Shutting Down in 2024?

August 29, 2024
blog

The year 2024 has seen widespread rumors regarding the possible shutdown of the popular battle royale game Fortnite.

These rumors have sparked concerns among the game's massive player base.

But an in-depth analysis, backed by factual data, can shed light on the truth behind these speculations. Luckily, Fan Arch has you covered.

The Rumors Explained

Background

The rumors of Fortnite shutting down have been fueled by various factors, including the layoff of approximately 830 employees by Epic Games in late 2023 and the subsequent increase in the prices of in-game currency, V-Bucks.

These circumstances have led many players to believe that the game is on the brink of closure.

Fact-Checking

Despite the widespread rumors, it is crucial to emphasize that the claims of Fortnite shutting down in 2024 are not true.

The game remains popular and profitable, with a massive player base and continued development efforts by Epic Games.

Analyzing the Game's Popularity

Player Base

Fortnite has witnessed a remarkable growth trajectory since its release in 2017. As of 2023, the game boasted over 400 million active players, and this number is projected to surpass 500 million by the end of 2024.

Also, there are over 650 million registered Fortnite players, with an average monthly player count of 230 million.

Chapter 5 Success

Chapter 5 of Fortnite has been particularly influential, leading to an all-time high player count of 5,471,208 during the release of the recent OG Season Chapter 4 Season 5. This resurgence has contributed significantly to dispelling the notion of the game's decline.

Financial Viability

Revenue and Profit Margin

Despite being over six years old, Fortnite continues to attract millions of daily players. However, the profit margin has been impacted by the popularity of the Creative mode, as Epic Games shares 40% of its net revenue with map creators.

Still, the Battle Royale mode remains the most profitable, assuring the game's sustained development.

Impact of Layoffs

While the layoffs at Epic Games may have raised concerns, it is important to note that Fortnite's development is not expected to be significantly affected.

The game's enduring popularity and profitability provide a solid foundation for continued support and updates.

Addressing the "Dying Game" Notion

Historical Context

It is not uncommon for successful games to face speculation about their decline, and Fortnite has been no exception.

Around 2020, there was a perception that the game was becoming stale, prompting the need for significant changes to maintain its appeal.

Ongoing Development

In response to the perceived stagnation, Epic Games has introduced substantial changes with each new chapter, including new skins and Points of Interest (POIs).

Chapter 5, in particular, has garnered immense popularity, with experts predicting a 100 million jump in the number of active players in 2024.

 

On the whole, the rumors of Fortnite shutting down in 2024 are unfounded. The game's enduring popularity, massive player base, and ongoing development efforts by Epic Games provide strong evidence to refute these claims.

Despite challenges such as layoffs and perceived stagnation, Fortnite continues to thrive and remains a dominant force in the gaming industry.

s the year progresses, it is expected that further updates and developments will continue to shape the game's trajectory, ensuring its relevance and longevity in the gaming landscape.
