9 Visually Striking Animated Movies To Watch If You Liked Spider-Man: Across the Spider-Verse

July 13, 2024
blog

Spider-Man: Across the Spider-Verse was one of the most highly anticipated movies of 2023. Fans have been eagerly waiting for the continuation of Miles Morales’ story since the credits rolled on Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse. The immense hype and appreciation for the character have even sparked discussions of a potential live-action adaptation. While we impatiently await the next installment, here are nine animated movies that can help fill the void.

9. The Mitchells vs the Machines (2021)

“A road trip paired with a quirky family saving the world; what’s not to like?”


Nominated for an Oscar, The Mitchells vs the Machines is a special animated movie that appeals to families of all types. The film follows a quirky family on a road trip as they unexpectedly find themselves saving the world. It beautifully portrays the father-daughter relationship, delving into the complexities of understanding each other and ultimately forming a meaningful bond.

8. Next Gen (2018)

“There’s something about the pairing of a robot and a young person that really pulls on the heartstrings of audiences everywhere.”


Next Gen is set in a futuristic world where a young girl named Mai befriends an attack robot named 7723. Together, they form an unlikely bond and embark on an adventure that leads to great memories and tearful goodbyes. This film explores themes of friendship, making new memories, and the power of unexpected connections.

7. Trollhunters: Rise of the Titans (2021)

“If you crave the feeling that Spider-Man: Across the Spider-Verse gave you: the action, the found family trope, and likable heroes.”


Rise of the Titans concludes the beloved Trollhunters series on Netflix. It offers a thrilling blend of action, a found family dynamic, and endearing heroes. With a talented voice cast including Nick Offerman, Tom Kenny, and Diego Luna, this movie provides an exciting adventure that will satisfy your cravings for engaging storytelling.

6. The LEGO Batman Movie (2017)

“The cast for this incredibly fun and silly animated film is so great, with people you wouldn’t expect filling iconic roles.”


The LEGO Batman Movie features an all-star cast, led by Will Arnett as Batman and Michael Cera as Robin. This hilarious and enjoyable film explores Batman’s journey as he learns to be a family man and accepts the importance of teamwork in his crime-fighting endeavors. With unexpected casting choices and a playful tone, it offers a fresh take on the iconic superhero.

5. Raya and the Last Dragon (2021)

“There are truly beautiful animated moments that’ll sweep you up in the coloring and flow of the scene that deserve recognition.”


Raya and the Last Dragon is a visually stunning Disney movie that deserves more attention. It follows the young girl Raya as she embarks on a quest to find the last dragon, Sisu, and restore the dragon gem to save her father. The film’s fresh and distinct plot, coupled with captivating animation, creates an immersive experience for viewers.

4. Big Hero 6 (2016)

“Big Hero 6 will have you laughing at one moment and then wiping tears from your eyes in the scene directly after.”


Big Hero 6 tells the story of young Hiro, a genius navigating the loss of his big brother and finding his place in the world. With a lovable robot companion named Baymax, Hiro embarks on a journey of self-discovery, emotional healing, and creating bonds. This film strikes a balance between humor and heartfelt moments, captivating audiences throughout.

3. Next Avengers: Heroes of Tomorrow (2008)

“Within the Marvel Universe, there have been a collection of animated films that might have flown under your radar, so you haven’t been able to appreciate them yet.”


Next Avengers: Heroes of Tomorrow explores a unique storyline within the Marvel Universe. Set in a future where the original Avengers’ children are raised by Tony Stark, this film introduces a new generation of heroes. It offers surprising parent-child pairings and showcases the potential of these young heroes as they face their own challenges.

2. Kubo and the Two Strings (2016)

“Sometimes you need an authentic stop-motion adventure film, and you should give Kubo and the Two Strings a shot.”


Kubo and the Two Strings presents a captivating stop-motion adventure. The film follows Kubo, a young boy who embarks on a journey to confront villains and discover his own strength while processing grief and navigating complex emotions. The unique animation style enhances the storytelling and adds depth to the film’s themes.

1. Kirikou and the Sorceress (1998)

“Kirikou and the Sorceress is a visually stunning movie that focuses on African folklore with a very compelling story.”


Kirikou and the Sorceress is a visually striking animated movie rooted in African folklore. It tells the tale of Kirikou, a magical baby who embarks on a courageous journey to confront an evil sorceress and save his village. The film’s mesmerizing animation and exploration of compassion make it a must-watch for fans of unique and culturally rich storytelling.

Q: Which animated movie explores themes of family and bonding?

The LEGO Batman Movie (2017) beautifully explores themes of family and bonding. The film depicts the journey of Batman and Robin as they learn to work together and form a familial bond.

Q: Are there any animated movies with unique animation styles?

Kubo and the Two Strings (2016) stands out with its distinctive stop-motion animation style. The film’s visually stunning presentation adds depth to its storytelling and enhances the overall viewing experience.
