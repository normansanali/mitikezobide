Melanie Martinez 2024 Portals Tour Dates Revealed: Get Ready For a Musical Journey

September 22, 2024
blog

Melanie Martinez has announced an update of her “PORTALS Tour” which is slated to occur in 2024 in locations throughout Southeast Asia. The singer’s studio album of the same name will be supported by the tour. In this post, you will read about her album and tour.

What is Melanie Martinez 2024 Portals Tour Dates?

The new tour performances will be held in the three locations. She will perform at Toyosu Pit in Tokyo, Japan on January 19, 2024. Then, on January 23, 2024, she will travel to Manila, Philippines, where she will perform at the World Trade Center. Then, on January 25, 2024, she will travel to Singapore and do a performance at the Singapore Expo.

Fans in these cities have the opportunity to witness her play live and take in her music during her tour. The singer launched a significant world tour in promotion of the record, beginning with a North American leg that will conclude on October 21, 2023 with a performance at Monterrey, Mexico’s Parque Fundidora.

It will be followed by a leg of the singer’s European tour which will conclude her 2023 tour schedule. Her final performance is currently planned to take place at Camp Pequeno in Lisbon, Portugal on November 30, 2023.

The show’s current last tour leg, which continues until early February 2024 will begin in Asia in early January after Melanie Martinez finishes her stay in Europe. Thereafter, she will proceed to Oceania.

Where You Can Get Melanie Martinez’s Tour Tickets?

On October 18, 2023, presales for the tour will open up. To get them, you must first successfully register on the Live Nation website. On October 19, 2023, general tickets for the tour will go on sale through the aforementioned website. The cost of the tickets has not yet been disclosed.

Which Album of Melanie Martinez Has a Similar Name as Her Tour?

On March 31, 2023, Melanie Martinez released “Portals” her most recent studio album. The album has had significant success thus far, peaking at the top of the album charts in Canada and New Zealand. The album also reached its highest point at number two on the UK and Billboard 200 album rankings.
