If you are a NBA follower, then you must be aware of the mysterious tale of Brian Williams, more popular as Bison Dele. The former Chicago Bulls star suffered a horrific death, reportedly by the hands of his older brother, Miles Dabord, in 2002. 

The former central player from Orlando Magic was a NBA Champion for Chicago Bulls back in 1997. Today, we give you more details on the mysterious death of Bison Dele and much more.

Bison Dele Murdered by Brother 

The former NBA star, Bison Dele, was murdered by his bloodline, brother, Miles Dabord, in 2002. Dele, Dabord’s girlfriend, Serena Karlan, and skipper Bertrand Saldo sailed from Tahiti on his watercraft on July 6, 2002. Surprisingly, his brother was also on the ship according to the satellite phone calls made during that month. 

In the early days of the trip, basketball star Dele and Darlon were always in touch with their families. However, suddenly, the connection was lost. And finally, when the boat returned to Tahiti on July 20, 2002, there was only Dele’s brother, Miles Dabord, in the ship. 

Thus, that was it, the unfortunate disappearance of the NBA star, Bison Dele, Darlon, and skipper in the South Pacific ocean. 

Investigation on Bison Dele’s Unfortunate Murder

After the unfortunate murder of the retired Chicago Bulls man, Bison Dele, the police conducted a sting operation to find out the hidden truths behind the death. The cops caught Dabord on September 5, 2002, since he bought massive gold worth $152 thousand by using his brother’s name and a forged signature.

Finally, the police concluded that it was Dele’s brother Dabord who killed him, his girlfriend, and the captain. Since the boat, Hukuna Matata, had patched bullet holes and nameplate removed, police claimed that Dabord shot all three and threw the bodies in the ocean.

Dele’s brother Dabord blamed the departed NBA star, claiming that it was he who pushed his wife, Darlon, and the boat’s captain. Consequently, he had to kill him in the attempt of self-defense in his version of the story. 

Adding more twist to the tale, Dabord intentionally overdosed in insulin that put him in a coma. Eventually, he died at the California hospital on September 27, 2002.

Bison Dele and NBA

The late NBA star, Bison Dele started his NBA journey when he was the tenth pick in the first round of 1991 NBA draft for Orlando Magic. However, he couldn’t enjoy the two years in the squad since he didn’t get enough time on the court.

Dele’s NBA journey resumed with Denver Nuggets in 1993, where he played 80 games, his career highest in a single season. Later, he signed with the Los Angeles Clippers and averaged 15.8 points per game in his one year at the team. 

Dele Signed for Chicago Bulls to Create History

Following a contractual dispute, Dele was unable to find any team because his asking price was very high. In the latter part of the 1996-97 season, he struck a deal with Chicago Bulls, just nine games before the season end.

Bison Dele Retired at Prime Age of 30
