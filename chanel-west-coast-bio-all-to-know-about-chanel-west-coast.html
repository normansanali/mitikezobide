Who is Chanel West Coast?

Chelsea Chanel Dudley is an American television personality, rapper, and singer better known by her stage name Chanel West Coast. She rose to fame thanks to her parts in the MTV shows Ridiculousness and Rob Dyrdek’s Fantasy Factory.

Chanel West Coast bio

California’s Los Angeles is where Chanel was born. As a child, Chanel split her time between New York City and North Hollywood, where she lived with her mother, Penny Porsche, and her father, DJ Jamie Dudley, who took Chanel to nightclubs.

Early on, Chanel took singing and dancing lessons. At the age of 11, she started listening to rap music, and at 14, she started rapping.

Chanel West Coast career

At the age of 20, Chanel met Rob Dyrdek and began working for him as a receptionist while he concentrated on his reality show, “Rob Dyrdek’s Fantasy Factory.”

Chanel used this connection to begin acting on a variety of MTV shows, making her debut with “The Hard Times of RJ Berger,” and later began to appear with Dyrdek on “Ridiculousness,” a reality show where the hosts review online videos.

And finally, she made her debut appearance on “Fantasy Factory” in the fifth season of the program.

For a short period after that, she continued to feature on “Ridiculousness” and “Fantasy Factory,” and she also made a name for herself as a voice actor by landing a part on the animated series “Wild Grinders.”

She also secured a recurring part for “Love & Hip-Hop: Hollywood’s” fourth season in 2017.

Soon after starting her job with MTV, Chanel West Coast started her music career. She began working with a variety of other musicians in 2009, and Tiffanie Anderson helped her record her debut track, “Melting Like Ice Cream.”

She was signed by Lil Wayne to his record label, Young Money Entertainment, in 2012, and her subsequent notable songs included “PHAMOUS” and “I Love Money.” This resulted in the 2013 publication of her debut mixtape, “Now You Know.”

For this album, she worked with a variety of musicians, including Snoop Dogg, French Montana, Robin Thicke, and many more. The mixtape was subsequently marketed by Chanel with several tours.

“WAVES,” her second mixtape, is a collaboration with musicians including YG and B-Real. She made a studio album announcement in 2014 and started releasing songs like “Blueberry Chills” with Honey Cocaine.

The songs “Miles and Miles” and “Bass in the Trunk,” which would subsequently appear on Chanel’s debut studio album, were released repeatedly during the ensuing years.
